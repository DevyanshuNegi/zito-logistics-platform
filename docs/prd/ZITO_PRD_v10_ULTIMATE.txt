1. Product Vision
ZITO is a unified logistics super platform built for Africa-scale operations. It consolidates fragmented transport, warehousing, and last-mile delivery services into a single intelligent platform, enabling operators, transporters, drivers, warehouses, and customers to collaborate in real time.
Core Services
FTL (Full Truck Load) — long-haul freight transport
PTL / LCL (Partial Load) — consolidated cargo shipments
Courier / Last-mile delivery — direct-to-door delivery
Warehouse & storage services — managed storage with full inventory control
Containerized trucking — explicit 20ft and 40ft container movement
Rail / SGR corridor logistics — container rail movement such as Mombasa port to inland rail-linked nodes
Fleet & transporter management — vehicle lifecycle and driver coordination
Multi-country logistics — Africa-ready, offline-capable operations
2. 👤 User & Role System
ZITO supports multiple user types within a single unified identity. One account may hold multiple roles simultaneously, governed by the RBAC system.
User Roles
One user → multiple roles allowed. Permissions enforced at API and UI level.
Internal operations staffing rule:
- Super Admin and Admin are governance roles, not the only people allowed to operate the platform day to day
- internal operational execution must also support staffed departmental users under the Agency Staff / internal team model
- minimum internal departments required:
  - Operations staff: dispatch, booking control, fleet coordination, assignment handling
  - Customer care staff: user-request-driven support handling, ticket ownership, issue resolution, escalation to operations or accounts
  - Accounts staff: invoices, payments, refunds, reconciliation, accounting follow-up
- internal staff may be represented by the platform role `AGENCY_STAFF` with a department / desk classification such as `OPERATIONS`, `CUSTOMER_CARE`, or `ACCOUNTS`
- staff scope must also be modeled:
  - Head Office staff = centrally supervised operations, support, and finance teams
  - Agency Staff = branch / station / field teams tied to a specific agency
- internal provisioning UI must allow controlled creation of Customer, Corporate, Driver, Agent, Transporter, Courier Company, Warehouse Partner, Head Office Staff, and Agency Staff accounts from the admin workspace
- internally provisioned operational users may be activated directly by authorized admins for testing and operational readiness

Unified Login
All users authenticate through a single identity system, but not through one shared public app surface.

App split rule (implemented as 3 separate mobile apps):
Zito Logistics Service App = Individual Customer + Corporate
Zito Partners App = Driver + Agent + Transporter + Courier Company + Warehouse Partner
Zito Internal Ops = Super Admin + Admin + Head Office Staff
Zito Agency Desk = Agency Staff
Customers and partners must not see internal-role registration or internal login entry points in their public apps.
Internal roles must use a separate private login path and separate internal web/app experience.
Agent is an external supply-side partner role, separate from Agency / Agency Staff. Agents source vehicles and loads for commission, onboard drivers, manage supply capacity, and operate through Zito Partners.
Agency / Agency Staff remains an internal operating structure and must not be exposed in the public partner login or registration flow.
Internal teams must use separate private internal routes and must not be mixed into customer or partner login entry points.
Internal staff who handle support, operations, finance, and accounting must authenticate into an internal workspace, even when they are not platform admins.
Agency staff must authenticate through a dedicated agency login entry point, separate from the head-office/internal login entry point.
The mobile route tree must mirror this product partition:
- Zito Customer mobile = Customer
- Zito Driver mobile = Driver
- Zito Partners mobile = Agent + Transporter + Courier Company + Warehouse Partner
- Zito Internal Ops mobile = Super Admin + Admin + Head Office Staff
- Zito Agency Desk mobile = Agency Staff
identifier = phone OR email
Login Methods
Phone OTP (primary method)
Email OTP (secondary method)
Email Password (required after successful Email OTP verification)
Account Lifecycle
pending → verified → active → suspended → rejected
Rule: Only ACTIVE accounts may log in. Any other status results in a blocked session with a descriptive error.
Implemented Login Experience:
The sign-in experience must be Zito-only. Do not show legal-entity branding, customer-company names, or contract-party labels on the login screen.
Company/legal names belong in contracts, invoices, approval records, and formal documents, not on the public sign-in interface.
The auth screen must feel utility-first and professional, not like a marketing landing page.
Only one Zito identity should be presented per viewport section; avoid repeated or conflicting logo and wordmark combinations in the same auth surface.
OTP-first flow must remain on a single screen: contact step â†’ OTP step â†’ role-based redirect after successful verification.
UI must support phone-first sign-in with separate country-code and local-number fields, plus email sign-in as the secondary path.
The OTP step must show a masked verification target, change-contact action, resend countdown, and attempts-remaining messaging when the account is not yet locked.
The resend OTP action must remain visibly present on the OTP step even during cooldown, with disabled state plus countdown text rather than disappearing.
Canonical flow rule:
OTP step usability rule:
- the OTP screen must focus the code-entry field immediately on entry
- the OTP screen should auto-submit once a complete valid six-digit code is captured by paste, platform autofill, or compatible browser-assisted OTP capture unless the active compliance flow explicitly requires a separate final confirmation action
contact step -> OTP step for all public users -> password step only for email users -> role-based redirect after successful verification.
Phone login completes after OTP only.
Email login requires password after OTP verification and must not expose password entry on the initial contact step.
When email OTP trouble occurs and the account supports passwords, the same login screen must unlock email/password fallback without sending the user to a separate login page.
OTP Handling & Security Rules:
Target defaults:
OTP validity = 5 minutes
Resend cooldown = 30 seconds
UI must show resend countdown before another code can be requested
Maximum resend attempts = 4 resends after the first code, 5 total OTP requests
OTP validity: configurable (e.g., 2–5 minutes)
Resend OTP allowed after cooldown (e.g., 30 seconds)
Maximum resend attempts limit (e.g., 3–5 attempts)
Maximum verification attempts limit (e.g., 5 tries)
Rules:
Expired OTP must be rejected
Multiple failed attempts → temporary account lock
OTP must be single-use only
OTP must be stored hashed at rest (using bcrypt with 10 rounds)
OTP verification must use secure comparison (bcrypt.compare) to prevent timing attacks
Verification target must be masked in the UI
Failed OTP responses must show attempts remaining whenever the account is not yet locked
OTP provider failures must be distinguished from invalid credentials; provider-delivery failure, timeout, or upstream outage must surface as an integration / service-availability error, not as a false password or user-not-found response
Auth health monitoring must include OTP provider connectivity for SMS and email channels used in the active market
OTP entry surfaces must accept full-code paste and system autofill, not only one-digit manual entry
Phone-auth OTP UX should auto-fill wherever the platform supports it: iPhone through one-time-code autofill, Android through SMS-OTP or app-hash-compatible delivery, and compatible browsers through origin-bound WebOTP patterns where the SMS provider template supports it

Delivery OTP Rate Limiting:
- Applies to all delivery confirmation OTP attempts across the system
- Maximum 5 consecutive failed verification attempts per booking
- After 5 failed attempts, the booking enters a locked state for 15 minutes
- During lockout, any additional OTP verification attempts are rejected with lockout message
- Lockout is automatically cleared after 15 minutes
- Successful OTP verification resets the attempt counter to 0 and clears any existing lockout
- Rate limiting applies across all delivery verification paths: driver app status update and warehouse scan-based delivery confirmation
- Failed attempt counter increments regardless of verification method (driver status update vs warehouse scan)
Fallback:
Public self-service auth is split by app surface:
Zito Logistics Service App self-service roles: Individual Customer, Corporate
Zito Partners App self-service roles: Driver, Agent, Transporter, Courier Company, Warehouse Partner
Agency Staff and other internal roles must be provisioned through internal processes, not the public customer or partner registration flow.
Internal login paths must not be visible in the public customer or partner role-selection experience.
If OTP fails repeatedly, allow alternative login method (email/password) inside the same login flow when the account has password sign-in enabled
Phone sign-in must use separate country-code and local-number fields
If OTP fails repeatedly → allow alternative login method (email/password)

4. 📝 Signup & KYC Flow
phone → OTP → email → OTP → password → document upload → admin approval → active
Agent, Transporter, Courier Company, Corporate, and Warehouse Partner are B2B organization accounts. Their self-service registration must capture both company legal name and authorized contact person name.
Company name belongs to registration, approval, contracts, invoices, and internal records; it must not be asked for on the public login screen.
Documents required: National ID, Business Registration (Corporate / Agent / Courier Company / Warehouse Partner / Transporter), Driver's License (Driver), Vehicle Registration (Agent / Transporter).
Compliance & Re-Validation:
Driving license expiry tracking (drivers)
Vehicle insurance & permit expiry tracking (transporters)
System must trigger alerts before expiry (e.g., 15 days prior)
Expired documents → automatic suspension of driver/vehicle
Re-verification required before reactivation
Verification Control Rules:
- document upload does not equal verification; every uploaded document or photo pack must enter a pending_review state first
- an account, driver, transporter, courier company, warehouse partner, or fleet asset must not become active only because the first file was uploaded
- reviewer action must capture reviewer identity, action timestamp, verification status, and mandatory rejection reason when rejected
- verification workflow must support: pending_review -> approved -> rejected -> resubmission_required -> approved
- each document record must support document number or reference where applicable, issuing authority, country of issue, issue date, expiry date, uploaded timestamp, and reviewer notes
- the product must support front/back or multi-view capture where the document or inspection workflow requires more than one image
Role-Based Verification Matrix:
- Customer: phone OTP, email OTP or password completion path, National ID or Passport, and manual review only where policy, fraud risk, or regulated service requires it
- Corporate / Agent / Transporter / Courier Company / Warehouse Partner: company registration evidence, KRA PIN / tax identity where billing or contracting applies, authorized signatory ID, and approval of the business profile before activation
- Driver: National ID, Driving License, live driver photo or selfie for identity confirmation, expiry tracking, and approval before the driver can become dispatch-eligible
- Transporter and Courier Company: both organization verification and operational verification must exist; company approval alone is not enough to make vehicles or drivers eligible for live dispatch
- Fleet-capable accounts must verify every managed vehicle and every managed driver separately before those assets can be used on bookings
Kenya Logistics Verification Context:
- Kenya logistics, import, export, and cross-border operations are document-led and multi-agency; Zito must not model verification as a simple one-file upload
- where the booking type touches import, export, container, rail, port, ICD, or regulated cargo flow, the platform must support trade-document verification checkpoints in addition to user KYC
- compliance teams must be able to review and hold a shipment, user, or vehicle because of missing documentation, customs state, permit state, inspection expiry, or mismatch between uploaded records and the actual asset

Delivery OTP Security (Enhanced Security Control):
- OTP must be generated as 4-digit code and hashed using bcrypt (10 rounds) before storage in database
- OTP must never be stored in plain text at rest
- OTP verification must use bcrypt.compare() for time-safe comparison, not plain-text string matching

- Rate limiting applies to all delivery OTP verification endpoints: booking status update and warehouse scan confirmation

Rules:

- M-Pesa collection must support provider lifecycle states for STK push request, callback receipt, status-query request, status-query result, reversal request, reversal result, and provider timeout handling even where the finance UI still maps those into a smaller business-state set

Integration:

- M-Pesa integration must persist merchant request ID, checkout request ID, receipt number, conversation IDs, callback payload, async result payload, and timeout payload on the payment record for later reconciliation
- M-Pesa disbursement and settlement must persist beneficiary identity, selected rail, provider mode, provider status, provider receipt number, conversation IDs, result payloads, timeout payloads, initiated timestamp, processed timestamp, and reversed timestamp on a dedicated disbursement ledger record
- M-Pesa status sync must prefer STK query while a checkout request is active and must fall back to transaction-status workflow when a receipt exists but final reconciliation is still pending
- reversal requests must be available to admin / super-admin finance control and must not silently flip payment state without either provider result confirmation or an explicitly simulated non-production mode
- B2C and B2B payout initiation must support explicit finance selection of payout rail, beneficiary reference, and purpose and must not be collapsed into the customer collection workflow
- support integration with Kenyan banking APIs, file-based settlement, or reconciliation import for EFT, RTGS, and instant-bank-transfer rails
- support multi-currency settlement via bank channels where country rollout requires it, while Kenya launch remains KES-first

- all M-Pesa transactions must be logged with request payload, callback payload, provider receipt, and reconciliation outcome

19.1 Kenya Rail Booking Model
Kenya train booking exists in two distinct operational patterns and the product must treat them differently:
- passenger rail booking = schedule search -> passenger details -> payment -> confirmation code / ticket
- freight rail booking = corridor selection -> container / cargo details -> documentation & customs readiness -> rail allocation -> ICD / final-mile handoff
Rules:
- Zito Rail / SGR booking must follow the freight rail model, not the passenger e-ticket model
- Rail / SGR must be modeled as a corridor logistics service for freight and container movement such as Port of Mombasa -> ICD Nairobi, Port of Mombasa -> ICD Naivasha, ICD -> Port return, and other enabled rail-linked inland nodes
- rail booking must support import, export, domestic repositioning, and transit / corridor use cases where commercially enabled
- the booking request must capture at minimum:
  - corridor / lane
  - container size or cargo class
  - trade mode / direction of movement (local, import, export, transit)
  - origin node and destination node
  - first-mile / last-mile truck requirement
  - consignor and consignee details
  - clearing agent or documentation owner where applicable
- the first booking pass must at minimum persist trade mode, rail corridor code, origin node, destination node, container reference where applicable, bill of lading number, IDF reference, PAC readiness, customs status, and iCMS status
- domestic/local rail or container jobs may skip customs references, but import, export, and transit jobs must not
- rail scheduling must support regular train departures rather than only on-demand dispatch logic
- rail service availability must support corridor calendars, cutoff times, and booking-acceptance windows
- rail pricing must support corridor tariffs, container-size bands, inland node differentials, and optional first-mile / last-mile trucking add-ons

63. Full Warehouse Management & Booking System
Objective
The warehouse module is not just storage. It is a full operational system covering customer warehouse booking, inbound appointment scheduling, receiving, inventory control, picking, packing, dispatch, cold chain, value-added services, reporting, and billing.
63.1 Warehouse Types & Storage Modes
Supported warehouse modes:
- Ambient / Dry Store: standard temperature storage for FMCG, general cargo, and electronics.
- Cold Store (2-8 C): refrigerated storage for pharmaceuticals, perishables, and dairy.
- Frozen Store (-18 C): deep-freeze storage for frozen food and sensitive temperature-controlled goods.
- Bulk / Floor Storage: open floor allocation for pallets, heavy goods, and oversized items.
- Bonded Warehouse: customs-controlled inventory for import/export handling.
- Open Yard: secured outdoor area for containers, vehicles, and oversized cargo.
Every warehouse location must declare:
- storage modes supported
- zone-level capacity
- operating hours
- service radius
- dock count and dock type
- special handling capability (hazmat, cold chain, bulky, high-value)
Warehouse physical hierarchy must support:
- Warehouse -> Zone -> Rack -> Row or Shelf -> Bin
- every bin must be uniquely addressable and queryable in real time
Minimum bin master data:
- bin_id
- zone_id
- rack_id
- row_or_shelf_ref
- storage_type
- max_weight_kg
- max_volume_cbm
- occupancy_status
- current_owner_id
- current_sku
- current_quantity
Additional structure rules:
- zone types may include Dry, Cold, Bonded, Open Yard, and Quarantine with separate pricing where commercial rules require it
- warehouse operations must expose a visual rack/bin map for occupancy visibility
- one owner per bin remains the default enforcement rule; any shared-bin exception requires explicit product and contract enablement plus auditable segregation controls
63.2 Customer Warehouse Booking Portal
Customers, Corporates, and Courier Companies must be able to self-book warehouse space from the app or portal without a manual phone workflow.
Booking flow:
- user selects Warehouse and Storage from service menu
- system shows eligible warehouses on map with live availability
- user selects storage type, expected quantity or volume, weight, inbound date and time, expected storage duration, and handling needs
- system checks real-time capacity and available inbound slot availability
- booking is confirmed with a Warehouse Booking Reference (WBR)
- system issues inbound appointment slot, access instructions, and QR or digital reference for gate check-in
Minimum warehouse booking fields:
- storage type
- goods description
- quantity and/or volume
- weight
- inbound date and time
- expected storage duration
- special handling requirements
- insurance required flag
- associated transport booking, LR, or waybill where applicable
Warehouse discovery and commercial rules:
- warehouse search must support filters for location, storage type, available capacity, price, and partner rating
- booking quote must be visible before confirmation
- booking confirmation may depend on advance payment, credit approval, or contracted billing terms
63.3 Dock & Inbound Appointment Scheduling
Each warehouse must support structured dock-slot scheduling.
Rules:
- each warehouse defines number of docks, dock type, operating hours, and slot capacity
- inbound and outbound slots are exclusive and cannot be overbooked
- slot availability is visible in real time to customer and warehouse operations
- arriving vehicles trigger check-in, driver verification, dock assignment, and GRN initiation
- outbound dispatch must support separate appointment scheduling
- no-show slot grace period is configurable; default policy is 30 minutes before release
Inbound appointment fields must support:
- date
- time window
- vehicle type
- pallet or unit count
- driver contact
Outbound release requests must support:
- requested SKU list
- quantities
- delivery address
- preferred dispatch date
Partner actions:
- warehouse partner may accept, reschedule, or reject an appointment with reason
- configurable no-appointment policy may reject unannounced vehicles
63.4 Goods Receiving Note (GRN) System
Every inbound warehouse movement must generate a GRN.
GRN lifecycle:
initiated -> in_progress -> completed -> discrepancy_raised -> closed
GRN process:
- vehicle checks in at gate or dock
- warehouse operator starts receiving workflow
- all cartons, pallets, parcels, or units are scanned during offload
- received quantity is compared to expected manifest or booking
- damage, shortage, overage, and mismatch are flagged immediately
- receiving operator captures evidence and signs off digitally
- accepted goods are allocated to storage location or cross-dock flow
Minimum GRN fields:
- GRN ID
- WBR / LR / Waybill reference
- goods owner / customer
- expected quantity
- received quantity
- condition on receipt
- receiving operator ID
- receiving timestamp
- discrepancy notes
- photo proof where required
- assigned storage bin or dispatch destination
Discrepancies must automatically raise:
- loss detection event
- warehouse issue / support event
- claim workflow input where damage or shortage is present
GRN completion controls:
- GRN PDF must include GRN number, warehouse, customer, SKU list, quantities, condition notes, receiver signature, and timestamp
- signed GRNs must become audit-locked after completion
- short, excess, and damaged receipt variance must be preserved as separate discrepancy categories
63.4.1 Damage At GRN And Quarantine Control
When damage is detected during GRN:
- receiver must classify damage type, capture photo evidence, and record affected quantity
- affected inventory must move to a Quarantine Zone state until resolved
- customer must be notified immediately with report and evidence
- customer options must include accept with note, raise claim, or request return / reverse movement
- quarantine overrides require mandatory admin reason logging
- quarantine aging must be reported, with items older than 14 days flagged for escalation
Damage claim SLA baseline:
- initial response target: 48 hours
- full resolution target: 7 days unless contract overrides apply
63.5 SKU & Product Catalog Management
Repeated warehouse clients must be able to maintain a SKU catalog.
Capabilities:
- customer creates SKU code, product name, category, UOM, dimensions, weight, storage rule, and expiry behavior
- inbound GRN may reference SKU by barcode or manual match
- stock levels are tracked per SKU by warehouse, zone, rack, and bin
- reserved, available, on-hand, damaged, and in-transit quantities are separated
- minimum stock thresholds generate alerts
- ownership visibility is limited to the owning client and authorized warehouse staff
Default SKU data must also support:
- barcode / QR / EAN reference

63.6 Bin Location & Storage Assignment
Storage hierarchy:
Warehouse -> Zone -> Rack -> Shelf -> Bin
Rules:
- every bin stores capacity by unit, weight, or volume
- temperature and storage constraints are declared at zone/bin level
- bin suggestion may be automatic based on capacity, FIFO, FEFO, and storage rules
- manual override requires reason logging
- multi-client inventory is allowed only with ownership separation enforced
- no untracked storage movement is allowed; every movement must be scanned and auditable
Put-away execution rules:
- staff must scan both bin and item to confirm put-away

66. App Design System & UX Specification
Objective
The product experience must be defined as rigorously as the operations engine. Zito must feel like a modern logistics product, not a data-entry portal. The UI model is map-first, card-native, action-forward, and mobile-credible, with standards strong enough to outperform industrial benchmarks such as Porter-style booking flows while remaining Africa-ready and offline-aware.
66.1 The Core Design Problem - Forms vs Cards vs Map
The booking experience must not default to long forms. The map is the interface; cards and bottom sheets are the primary interaction surfaces.
Required design shifts:
- pickup and drop are map pin or search-overlay actions, not plain text form blocks on the first screen
- vehicle selection is visual card-based with live pricing, not a dropdown
- live pricing must show the location scope being applied when county, town, or rural pricing rules materially affect the quote
- when map/geocode data is available, pricing scope should default from the pickup pin automatically and surface a clear restore action if the user manually overrides it
- cargo details are progressive and mostly optional unless operationally mandatory
- booking confirmation is a concise summary card or bottom sheet, not a separate paperwork-style page
- booking history is card feed first; table views are secondary and report-oriented
66.2 Design Language - The Zito System
Core design principles:
- Map-first: every location-heavy workflow begins from the map
- Card-native: content appears in cards, tiles, chips, sheets, and status trails rather than grids of raw data
- Action-forward: the primary action is always visually obvious
- Progressive disclosure: ask only for what is needed now
- One-thumb design: primary mobile controls remain in the lower interaction zone
- Offline-tolerant: every critical screen has a meaningful degraded state
66.3 Color System
Required semantic palette:
- Zito Navy: primary brand, navigation, trust layer, primary headings
- Zito Amber: urgency, highlights, recommendations, caution states
- Surface White / light surfaces: cards, sheets, modal backgrounds
- Background Blue / muted surface blue: app background and inactive layers
- Text Primary: headings and body
- Text Secondary: labels, timestamps, hints
- Success Green: confirmed, paid, online, delivered
- Alert Red: failed, cancelled, SOS, overdue
- Info Blue: route lines, info markers, informational states
- Pickup Pin color and Delivery Pin color must remain visually distinct everywhere
Rules:
- color is never the only status signal; label and/or icon must accompany it
- dark brand colors should not dominate full-screen content backgrounds where readability suffers
66.3A Brand and Logo Harmonization
The operational product experience must use restrained brand application. The HTML mobile reference clarifies the expected rule set:
- the working app should feel navy / white / cool-grey first, with amber used as accent rather than as a dominant fill
- operational screens should prefer a compact wordmark, small icon, or top-bar brand token instead of a large marketing lockup
- the full colorful Zito logo lockup belongs mainly on splash, onboarding, authentication, marketing, pitch, or help surfaces, not repeated inside every working mobile screen
- mobile top chrome should harmonize with Zito Navy backgrounds and white text, rather than stacking oversized logo art above operational content
- logo presentation must never visually fight with card content, map content, or CTA hierarchy

- booking creation should begin with route intent and map context; large dark summary blocks must not push the route setup below the fold on mobile
- route capture must be map-first: tapping pickup or drop must move the user into pin placement on the map, and manual address editing should only unlock after the pin has been dropped and reverse-filled
- interim free-map implementation is allowed using OpenStreetMap tiles / embed plus free geocoding, provided the UI still behaves like a premium mobile service flow
- if owned-fleet capability is enabled for the customer role, customer home or account must expose a clear Own Fleet entry so vehicle and driver management is discoverable without admin assistance
66.8 Screen Specification - Driver App
  Driver app mandatory experience:
  - the driver product must use a dedicated mobile driver shell; it must not inherit the internal PortalShell / workspace shell used by admin, staff, warehouse, or back-office surfaces
  - the driver shell must follow the same approved login-page palette family as the service app: deep navy header chrome, white cards, cool slate surfaces, restrained gradient emphasis, and the real Zito logo asset in compact form
  - the driver shell must feel like a field-operations mobile app, not a browser-based console wrapped onto a small screen
  - Driver Home: demand heatmap, online toggle, earnings strip, incoming-job card, no forms
  - Driver Home composition must mirror the approved mobile reference: top earnings strip, online / availability control, map or heatmap canvas, high-demand indicator, incoming job alert card, and persistent bottom navigation
  - Driver Home must not begin with KPI tables, admin cards, or portal headings before the operational map layer
  - Driver Home must not begin with a marketing hero, long descriptive paragraphs, or portal-style explainer copy; earnings strip and operational state must appear before any supporting copy
  - on the first meaningful driver-app launch, the product must request foreground location access with clear operational copy explaining why dispatch, heatmap placement, and live tracking need it
  - if location permission is denied, the driver app must show a non-blocking but prominent retry / settings path; silent failure is forbidden
  - when location permission is granted, the driver app must sync current coordinates to the driver profile endpoint and surface last-sync status in the UI
  - demand / zone setup warnings must be absorbed into the map or demand card as compact inline guidance; they must not dominate the entire driver home with a blocking alert unless the product is unusable
  - Driver Trips / Jobs page must use a card feed with route summary, status badge, and action controls; raw tables are forbidden as the primary mobile presentation
  - Driver Trips / Jobs page must use a compact queue header and status chips; oversized hero banners and generic UX slogans are forbidden on this route
  - Active Job / Navigate: map-led navigation, destination card, checklist, call and SOS actions
  - Delivery Confirmation: camera-first POD flow, OTP capture, optional signature, no text-heavy completion screen
  - Earnings Dashboard: chart-first earnings view with payout action
  - Profile & Documents: compliance cards with clear status and upload actions
  - the fourth driver bottom tab must behave as Profile, even when the first implementation groups profile and shift controls on the same route
  - Driver Profile must include a Fleet entry point that opens the assigned-vehicle view; fleet must not be hidden or implied only through a status chip
  - the driver fleet view is a secondary route under Profile and must surface assigned vehicle identity, readiness, status, and payload context in mobile cards
  - shift controls and recent shift history belong inside the driver profile surface as mobile cards; raw tables are forbidden for shift history on the driver app
  - any secondary driver route outside the main bottom-tab destinations must render a clear back control in the top chrome; primary bottom-tab pages should not rely on browser back as their only navigation affordance
  - the driver experience should favor job-alert cards, trip cards, payout cards, and checklist cards over generic form blocks or dense operational tables
66.9 Screen Specification - Admin Portal (Web)
Admin portal principles:
- dashboard is card-first and operations-first
- tables are secondary, mostly for report mode, export mode, or detailed audit drill-down
- live map, KPI cards, activity feeds, filters, and action panels are preferred primary surfaces
- even internal dashboards should harmonize with the same navy / white / muted-surface system rather than drifting into unrelated visual themes
Required admin modules must feel like:
- Operations Dashboard with KPI cards, live map, and activity feed
- Booking Management with booking cards, route preview map, live assignment actions, and inline status / cancellation controls
- Driver Assignment Panel with proximity-aware driver cards
- User Management with expandable user cards and side-panel creation
- Fleet Overview with card grid and compliance RAG status
- Analytics / Reports with chart-first presentation
- Assignment Engine with rule cards and mode toggles rather than form pages
Required internal-workforce capabilities:
- Admin Users & Teams workspace must create Customer, Corporate, Driver, Agent, Transporter, Courier Company, Warehouse Partner, Head Office Staff, and Agency Staff accounts
- Admin Marketplace workspace must surface Courier Company as a first-class partner type beside Agent, Transporter, and Warehouse Partner
- Admin and Super Admin must both be able to onboard courier companies, review their coverage, approve or suspend them, and publish courier opportunities to them from the same internal control layer
- Admin and Super Admin must both have a dedicated Courier Company Control desk that combines account provisioning, account status control, marketplace approval state, owned-fleet visibility, and finance exposure in one internal workspace
- Admin and Super Admin must both have a dedicated Warehouse Partner Control desk that combines account provisioning, warehouse-partner approval state, managed-site visibility, capacity context, and finance exposure in one internal workspace
- Admin and Super Admin must both have a dedicated Workforce Control desk that separates Head Office staff from Agency Staff and allows direct creation, activation, suspension, and department assignment for Operations, Customer Care, and Accounts teams
- Admin and Super Admin must both have a dedicated Verification Control desk covering customer KYC, driver approval, partner approval, document review, fleet verification, expiry monitoring, rejection handling, and resubmission tracking
- the Verification Control desk must support fleet-photo review and must clearly show whether a truck is missing dashboard, front, right-side, left-side, or rear photo evidence
- Admin and Super Admin must both have a dedicated Billing Control desk that combines platform-fee invoices, warehouse invoices, corporate invoice consolidation, and inter-agency settlement generation in one finance workspace
- Admin and Super Admin must both have a dedicated Capacity Planning desk covering warehouse occupancy, fleet readiness, booking-capacity validation, and short-horizon forecast signals in one operations workspace
- Admin and Super Admin must both have a dedicated Notifications desk that exposes the operator inbox, unread acknowledgements, and notification-to-entity context without forcing staff back through unrelated modules
- Admin and Super Admin must both have a dedicated SLA Control desk showing live timers, active breaches, escalation states, and no-show automation controls
- Admin and Super Admin must both have a dedicated Surge Pricing desk showing zone-level demand pressure, peak-hour rules, multiplier recommendations, and direct activate/deactivate controls
- Admin and Super Admin must both have a dedicated Heatmap Control desk showing zone intensity, demand-vs-supply ratio, and threshold-management controls for low / medium / high classification
- Admin and Super Admin must both have a dedicated Retention desk showing customer repeat-rate signals, loyalty / promo readiness, and driver-referral pipeline visibility
- Admin and Super Admin must both have a dedicated Route Optimization desk covering manual route simulation, booking-route inspection, ETA review, and route-deviation checks from the same internal control layer
- Admin and Super Admin must both have a dedicated Rail & Container Control desk covering freight corridor queue, customs-state review, PAC readiness, iCMS state, bill-of-lading and IDF capture, container reference control, and node-to-node handoff visibility for port / ICD / warehouse workflows
- KYC review must happen at document level first, and the account status must then be recalculated from the reviewed document set:
  - approved required set => verified
  - missing or pending required documents => pending
  - rejected required document => rejected
  - activation remains a separate internal step after verification
- owned-fleet and transporter fleet workspaces must expose the truck-photo upload workflow directly so operators can submit missing dashboard, front, right-side, left-side, and rear evidence without waiting for back-office entry
- when creating Agency Staff or Head Office Staff, the creator must choose department / desk classification:
  - Operations
  - Customer Care
  - Accounts
- when creating staff, the creator must also choose operating scope:
  - Head Office
  - Agency
- Head Office staff must route into the head-office desk and Agency Staff must route into the agency desk after sign-in
- Customer Care desk must be request-led:
  - users raise support requests first
  - customer care staff then view the queue, assign to self, resolve directly, or escalate to operations/accounts
- Operations desk must be able to view and manage platform bookings, trip status, assignment, and live operational workload
- Accounts desk must be able to view and handle invoices, payments, refunds, and finance-control actions without requiring full admin status
- Admin remains a supervisory control role, not the sole operator for daily dispatch, support, or finance work
- Admin dashboard must surface customer and partner-network visibility including transporter, courier company, warehouse partner, and staff footprints
- Admin bookings workspace must be card-first and map-first in live operations mode; wide audit tables may exist only as secondary report views, not as the primary dispatch surface
- rail and container bookings must show freight-specific operational context inside the admin booking workspace and must deep-link into the Rail & Container Control desk when customs or corridor control is required
66.10 Navigation Patterns
  Customer and Driver app navigation rules:
  - bottom tab navigation with 4 tabs maximum
  - icon above label
  - persistent visibility
  - active tab clearly highlighted
  - badges used for active bookings and unread notifications where relevant
  - driver primary tabs must behave like a mobile app tab bar, not like portal sidebar links reproduced at the top of the page
  - driver primary tabs should resolve to Jobs, Earnings, Trips, and Profile in the default mobile experience
  - secondary driver pages such as heatmap detail, document detail, or deep trip workflow screens must use a compact back action in the top bar
  Anti-patterns forbidden by PRD:
  - hamburger-menu-first mobile navigation
  - full-page booking forms for core user flows
  - dropdown-led vehicle selection
  - admin-style tables as primary mobile view
  - internal workspace chrome reused directly inside customer or driver mobile apps
  - raw status code strings shown to end users
  - blank error pages where inline contextual empty/error states are possible
Mobile platform coverage and release rules:
- the shared mobile codebase must support both Android and iPhone; no user-critical workflow may be Android-only unless explicitly approved as a temporary exception
- iPhone builds must be testable during development through Expo-compatible workflows and distributable for QA through TestFlight before public App Store release
- Android pre-release testing may use internal APK/AAB distribution, but iPhone pre-release testing must use a managed Apple distribution path such as TestFlight or equivalent approved enterprise/internal distribution
- iPhone configuration must include explicit permission copy for location, camera, and photo-library access where those capabilities are used for booking, tracking, POD, scans, or support evidence
- the mobile release process must maintain parity across Android and iPhone for customer, driver, transporter, and courier-company role workflows
- the mobile release process must maintain parity across Android and iPhone for customer, driver, agent, transporter, courier-company, warehouse-partner, internal-ops, and agency-desk role workflows
- the shared mobile route tree must expose dedicated role workspaces for Agent, Warehouse Partner, Internal Ops, and Agency Desk rather than collapsing those users into a generic customer or transporter shell
- Agent mobile must expose at minimum dashboard, marketplace opportunities, and partner-support surfaces
- Warehouse Partner mobile must expose at minimum dashboard, inventory, and warehouse-support surfaces
- Internal Ops mobile must expose at minimum dashboard, alerts, and internal support-control surfaces for Admin, Super Admin, and Head Office Staff
- Agency Desk mobile must expose at minimum operations, accounts, and support surfaces for Agency Staff
- the repository must include explicit mobile release scripts and build profiles for Android internal QA, Android production Play submission, iPhone internal QA, and iPhone TestFlight/App Store submission; release preparation must not depend on undocumented manual terminal sequences
- the shared mobile environment template must document the runtime API host, maps key, and build-environment label required for Expo/EAS delivery, and the release guide must distinguish Windows-safe Android workflows from macOS-required local iPhone native workflows
- Android production release readiness requires app-bundle output and Google Play track submission support; iPhone release readiness requires TestFlight-capable EAS production build configuration even when the actual Apple credentials are managed outside the repository
- the mobile repository must use the correct Expo EAS CLI invocation path in its documented commands and scripts; ambiguous or incorrect bootstrap commands that fail on Windows shells must be replaced with a working project-local EAS CLI entrypoint
- mobile OTP verification screens must expose autofill-friendly inputs and support automatic distribution of a full six-digit OTP into the visible code cells when the operating system or clipboard provides the entire code at once
66.11 Performance Targets - User Visible
User-visible performance goals:
- cold start to home under defined mobile threshold

- address search results near-instant after user pause
- quote generation fast enough to preserve flow confidence
- booking confirmation never blank or ambiguous
- driver updates visible at predictable cadence
- bottom sheet and navigation animations remain smooth
- adjacent-tab data prefetch where practical

- no earnings yet
Rules:
- no blank white screens
- every empty state must provide context and next action

66.17 Product Hardening Before External Go-Live
Before company-registration-dependent integrations are finalized, the product must still be operationally strong, deployment-ready, and internally testable.
Deployment readiness rules:
- the current web delivery assumption is split frontend and backend deployment on Vercel; frontend and mobile runtime configuration must not retain obsolete Railway fallback hosts once Vercel deployment is active
- deployed frontend environments must expose an explicit backend origin when frontend and backend run as separate Vercel projects
- mobile runtime defaults may use the deployed Vercel backend origin for QA convenience, while local development may override that host explicitly through environment files
Freight and container operational depth rules:
- rail and container bookings must expose a visible milestone chain rather than hiding all freight progress inside a single booking status
- milestone coverage must include at minimum document pack readiness, customs clearance, PAC readiness, origin handoff, linehaul, ICD or destination gate handoff, last-mile dispatch, and delivery confirmation where relevant
- admin freight control desks must allow milestone status updates, notes, and blocked-state capture without leaving the booking control workflow
- customer-facing freight bookings must expose a readable milestone timeline so import/export and corridor customers can understand where the shipment is delayed or progressing
Autobot v2 support rules:
- support ticket creation must persist autobot confidence, suggested quick action, escalation desk, and suggested first-response guidance in addition to the matched article summary
- customer support creation should show the suggested desk and suggested reply posture before ticket submission so the workflow feels guided rather than form-driven
- internal support queues and ticket threads must surface autobot routing metadata clearly enough for customer care, operations, accounts, or compliance desks to take action without re-triaging from scratch
Compliance automation rules:
- verification dashboards must surface expiring approved documents, expired documents, overdue user reviews, overdue vehicle reviews, missing photo packs, and any users auto-suspended by compliance automation
- operational roles affected by expired mandatory compliance evidence may be auto-suspended according to policy, but those actions must be visible in the verification control desk
- verification control must support explicit automation refresh so compliance teams can rerun the latest scan before taking manual decisions
Admin workflow polish rules:
- the main admin dashboard must surface cross-desk priorities, not just isolated module links
- compliance backlog, automation alerts, escalated support pressure, and finance-routed support pressure must all be visible from the main command surface
- quick actions must include direct access to the verification desk and freight-control desk
Protected workspace session rules:
- admin, internal, partner, agency, and other protected workspaces must require a valid authenticated user and a valid access token together; stale user-only session remnants must not be treated as authorized access
- a `401 Unauthorized` response from a protected workspace API call must clear the broken session and redirect the operator back to the correct portal login surface instead of leaving the user on a half-rendered control page with only an inline unauthorized error
Mobile QA hardening rules:
- the internal mobile workspace must expose a QA diagnostics screen showing the active API host, runtime environment label, token presence, user-role routing context, and live backend health response
- mobile API clients must use a bounded request timeout and return actionable errors when the runtime cannot reach the deployed backend
- mobile documentation and environment templates must reflect the active deployment strategy and must not reference obsolete infrastructure defaults
- installed Android and iPhone builds must resolve the root app launch route correctly; opening the app from the launcher or root deep link scheme must never show an unmatched-route screen
- the root mobile launch path must always land on a valid auth entry or role-routed home flow
Registration and account-provisioning UX rules:
- any registration, account-creation, staff-provisioning, partner-provisioning, or driver-onboarding form that captures a phone number must capture country code and local number as separate visible inputs; hidden or assumed country code behavior is not acceptable
- phone entry defaults may be preselected per market, but the country code must remain user-editable in every registration and provisioning flow
- create-user, create-staff, create-partner-account, and create-driver actions must show inline success or failure feedback at the same point as the submit action, not only in distant page-level alerts
- successful provisioning flows must offer an immediate "Create another" continuation action so back-office teams can onboard multiple users without manual form reconstruction
